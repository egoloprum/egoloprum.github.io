People

Chinggis Khaan

Chinggis Khaan known as Genghis Khan, was a legendary Mongol warrior and conqueror. He rose from a humble background to become the founder of the Mongol Empire, one of the largest empires in history, known for its military tactics and administrative innovations. Genghis Khan's leadership and strategic brilliance left an indelible mark on world history.

Sukhbaatar Damdin

Sukhbaatar Damdin known as Sukhbaatar, was a key figure in Mongolia's fight for independence during the early 20th century. His leadership was pivotal in achieving Mongolian sovereignty, and he is celebrated as a national hero for his role in shaping modern Mongolia.

Natsagdorj Dashdorj

Natsagdorj Dashdorj was a Mongolian writer, poet, playwright, and journalist. He is considered the founder and most-widely read author of modern Mongolian literature, and an exponent of "socialist realism".

Choinom Ryenchi

Choinom Ryenchi was a Mongolian poet. He was born on 10 February 1936 in the Darkhan Sum of the Khentii Province and died in 1978. Choinom's poems are famous for their fearlessness and realism. Throughout his life, his poems were widely popular but never received any official recognition under communist Mongolia.

Gurragchaa Jugderdemid

Gurragchaa Jugderdemid is a Mongolian cosmonaut and military leader. He was the first Mongolian and second Asian to go into space. He also was Mongolia's Defense Minister from 2000 to 2004.

Zorig Sanjaasuren

Zorig Sanjaasuren was a Mongolian politician who played a prominent role in leading the country's 1990 democratic revolution. His supporters called him the "Golden Magpie of Democracy". Zorig was murdered on Friday, 2 October 1998, and it remained unsolved for 19 years.

Dagvadorj Dolgorsuren

Dagvadorj Dolgorsuren , also known as Asashōryū Akinori is a Mongolian former professional sumo wrestler (rikishi). In 2005, he became the first wrestler to win all six official tournaments (honbasho) in a single year. Over his entire career, he won 25 top division tournament championships, placing him fourth on the all-time list.

The HU

The HU is a Mongolian folk metal band formed in 2016. Incorporating traditional Mongolian instrumentation, including the morin khuur, the tovshuur, and throat singing, the band calls their style of music "hunnu rock". The HU's music is used in two video games from the Star Wars universe: Star Wars Jedi: Fallen Order (2019) and Star Wars Jedi: Survivor (2023).

Enkhiin-Od Mike Sharavjamts

Enkhiin-Od Mike Sharavjamts is a Mongolian college basketball player for the San Francisco Dons of the West Coast Conference. He is the first Mongolian citizen to earn a Division I athletic scholarship.

Céline Dessberg

Céline Dessberg is a young French and Mongolian musician. Between indie pop and soft rock music, she composes pieces on guitar, piano and Mongolian harp.

Interesting Facts

Ulaanbaatar

Ulaanbaatar, the capital and largest city of Mongolia, is the coldest capital city in the world

Mongolia

Mongolia is the 18th largest country in the world by area, but its population of just 3 million makes it the world's most sparsely populated country, with only about 2 people per square kilometer, which is 4.3 people per square mile.

Dinosaur

Mongolia is known for the discovery of dinosaur fossils, including the first-ever dinosaur eggs.

The Gobi Desert

The Gobi Desert, which spans southern Mongolia and northern China, is one of the largest deserts in the world.

Cashmere

Mongolia is world's second largest producer of Cashmere goat's wool, after China.

The Tavan Bogd

The Tavan Bogd Mountains in the Altai range are home to the tallest peaks in Mongolia, including Khuiten Peak, which stands at 4,374 meters (14,350 feet).

Camel

The two-humped Bactrian camel is native to Mongolia and is essential for transportation in the Gobi Desert

Snow leopards

A third of the world’s snow leopards reside in Mongolia’s mountains. There are between 500 and 1000 in Mongolia
